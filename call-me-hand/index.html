🤙 Call Me Hand

A hand with thumb and little (pinky) finger extended, making a traditional phone-like shape. May be used in conjunction with a face to suggest “call me” (please).

Not to be confused with the sign of the horns or the shaka sign (which does not yet have an emoji representation).

Call Me Hand was approved as part of Unicode 9.0 in 2016 and added to Emoji 3.0 in 2016.

Modifiers

The Call Me Hand emoji supports skin tone modifiers. A yellow (or other non-human) skin tone should be shown by default, unless an emoji modifier is applied.

  • 🤙🏻 Call Me Hand: Light Skin Tone
  • 🤙🏼 Call Me Hand: Medium-Light Skin Tone
  • 🤙🏽 Call Me Hand: Medium Skin Tone
  • 🤙🏾 Call Me Hand: Medium-Dark Skin Tone
  • 🤙🏿 Call Me Hand: Dark Skin Tone

Platforms without support for emoji modifiers display a missing symbol character (box with a cross, alien, or question mark in it) next to any modified emoji.
